**Document 1: Website Design Document**

Project: Seattle Venture Labs (SVL) Official Website  
Benchmark References: Alchemist Accelerator, Forum Ventures  
Target Launch: Q4 2025 (Readiness for March 2026 Batch)

## **1\. Executive Summary & Design Philosophy**

The Seattle Venture Labs website will serve as the digital gateway for high-potential international startups seeking US market entry. The design must balance the **institutional prestige** of Alchemist Accelerator with the **high-touch, community-driven feel** of Forum Ventures.

* **Core Aesthetic:** "Pacific Northwest Innovation." A clean, minimalist interface utilizing deep slate blues (Puget Sound), forest greens, and crisp white space. It should feel serious, expensive, and established.  
* **User Journey:** The site is designed to funnel two primary personas:  
  1. **International Founders:** Inform \-\> Inspire \-\> Apply.  
  2. **US Investors/Mentors:** Validate Quality \-\> Connect.

## **2\. Information Architecture (Sitemap)**

* **Home:** Hero Video (Seattle skyline/Global connections) → Value Prop → Portfolio Highlights → The Bridge Model → Footer.  
* **Program:**  
  * **The Model:** Narrative explanation of the cross-border bridge.  
  * **Curriculum:** The 6-Month Arc (March & August Batches).  
  * **Mentorship:** "The Fractional Co-founder" approach (Benchmarking Forum Ventures).  
* **Admissions (Apply):**  
  * **Criteria:** Who we invest in.  
  * **Application Portal:** Secure, multi-step form.  
  * **FAQ.**  
* **Community:**  
  * **The Forum (Login Required):** Private peer-to-peer and mentor interaction space.  
  * **Events:** Demo Days, Seattle Summits.  
* **Portfolio:** Interactive database of funded companies (filterable by country of origin and sector).  
* **About:** Mission, Leadership Team, Seattle Advantage.

## **3\. Key Functional Features**

### **A. The "Bridge" Application Portal (Benchmark: Alchemist)**

Unlike standard forms, this must handle complex international data.

* **Functionality:** Save-progress capability, video pitch upload, LinkedIn integration.  
* **Data Fields:** Incorporation status (Home country vs. Delaware C-Corp status), Visa requirements, Revenue in home market vs. US market.  
* **UX:** A sidebar progress tracker (similar to Alchemist’s intense application style) that conveys rigor.

### **B. SVL Connect "The Forum" (Benchmark: Forum Ventures)**

A gated community platform for active cohort members and alumni.

* **Features:**  
  * **Mentor Booking:** Calendar integration to book 1:1s with US VCs.  
  * **Resource Library:** Legal templates for "Flipping" (changing domicile to US), US hiring contracts, B2B sales scripts.  
  * **Peer Channels:** Slack-style discussion boards (e.g., \#visa-issues, \#fundraising-sf, \#enterprise-sales).

### **C. Interactive Portfolio Map**

A visual WebGL globe showing the "Origin" of the startup and their "Expansion" into the US.

* **Hover State:** Shows the startup's logo and "bridged" metric (e.g., "From Seoul to Seattle: $2M Seed Secured").

## **4\. UI/UX Guidelines**

* **Typography:**  
  * *Headings:* **Inter Tight** or **Helvetica Now Display** – Bold, confident, Swiss-style (nods to international neutrality).  
  * *Body:* **Satoshi** or **Roboto** – Highly readable for dense narrative content.  
* **Color Palette:**  
  * *Primary:* Midnight Navy (\#0A192F), Mist Grey (\#8892B0).  
  * *Accent:* Signal Green (\#64FFDA \- for "Apply" buttons/Success metrics) and Brick Red (subtle nod to historic Seattle architecture).  
* **Imagery:** Avoid generic "people shaking hands" stock photos. Use high-contrast black and white photography of the Seattle skyline, shipping ports (symbolizing trade), and candid shots of intense whiteboard sessions.

## **5\. Technology Stack Recommendation**

* **Frontend:** Next.js (React) for speed and SEO (crucial for global discovery).  
* **CMS:** Sanity.io or Contentful (allows easy updating of batch dates and portfolio).  
* **Backend:** Node.js/Supabase for the Application Portal and Forum user management.  
* **Hosting:** Vercel (Edge network ensures fast load times in Asia/Europe).

## **2\. Program Page: The 6-Month Crucible**

*Benchmarking Alchemist’s Curriculum Rigor*

The Philosophy:  
We believe that standard acceleration is insufficient for international startups. You do not need a class on how to pitch; you need a strategic overhaul of your narrative for the American ear. Our program is a six-month residency designed to strip away the localized constraints of your business model and rebuild it for infinite scalability.  
Phase I: The Cultural & Legal Flip (Weeks 1-8)  
The journey begins not with sales, but with structure. In your first eight weeks, you will sit with our legal architects to execute the "Flip"—establishing your Delaware C-Corp and safeguarding your IP. Simultaneously, we dismantle your pitch. We move you away from the modest, traction-based narratives common in Europe and Asia, and push you toward the vision-based storytelling that Silicon Valley investors demand. You will learn to speak the language of "Unicorn potential" rather than "sustainable growth."  
Phase II: US Market Immersion (Weeks 9-16)  
This is where the theory ends and the friction begins. You will not be emailing customers; you will be deployed into the US enterprise ecosystem. Leveraging the Forum Ventures benchmark of "fractional co-founding," our partners work alongside you to secure your first five US pilot contracts. We utilize Seattle’s proximity to giants like Microsoft and Amazon to force-multiply your network. You are expected to be on the ground, shaking hands, and closing deals that validate your cross-border thesis.  
Phase III: The Fundraising Circuit (Weeks 17-24)  
By the final phase, you are no longer an "international startup"—you are a US company with global DNA. We curate exclusive, closed-door sessions with partners from Sand Hill Road and Seattle’s top firms. This is not a public demo day of theater; these are high-stakes boardroom meetings arranged for founders who have proven their metrics. The program culminates in a transition to our Alumni Forum, ensuring your support network remains active long after the term sheet is signed.

## **3\. Admissions Page (2026 Batches)**

*Benchmarking Alchemist’s Exclusivity*

Who We Are Looking For:  
We are seeking the outliers who feel constrained by their geography. We invest in technical founders who have built complex solutions—often in B2B SaaS, Deep Tech, or Logistics—and have already demonstrated traction in their home country. We are less interested in "ideas" and more interested in "proven engines" that need a larger fuel tank.  
The 2026 Cohort Schedule:  
The doors to Seattle Venture Labs open twice a year.

* **The March 2026 Batch:** Designed for founders ready to capitalize on Q1/Q2 enterprise budget cycles. Applications open in late 2025\. This cohort focuses heavily on enterprise sales alignment and typically graduates during the peak of the Fall fundraising season.  
* **The August 2026 Batch:** Tailored for deep-tech and longer cycle startups. This cohort uses the quiet winter months to build product velocity, emerging in early Spring 2027 ready to dominate the market.

Application Process Narrative:  
Our application is rigorous by design. We ask you to detail not just your victories, but your "near-death" moments. We require a video introduction because we need to feel your energy and your command of English—the lingua franca of global business. Expect a multi-stage interview process where we challenge your assumptions about the US market. We accept less than 2% of applicants because we dedicate 100% of our resources to those who make the cut.

## **4\. About Us: The Seattle Advantage**

Why Seattle?  
While Silicon Valley is the bank, Seattle is the engine room. This city builds the infrastructure that runs the modern world. For an international founder, Seattle offers a unique strategic advantage: it is the geographic midpoint between the capital markets of the East and the innovation hubs of Asia. It is a city of builders, not just talkers. By basing your US operations here, you gain access to the same talent pool that built AWS and Azure, while maintaining a cost of operation that allows your seed capital to extend significantly further than in the Bay Area.

## **5\. The Forum (Community Feature Description)**

The Alumni Network:  
Access to Seattle Venture Labs does not end at graduation. You gain lifetime access to "The Forum," our proprietary digital ecosystem. This is where the hive mind lives. Imagine a space where a founder in Tokyo can instantly query a peer in New York about visa complexities, or where you can download the exact hiring contract used by a unicorn alumni to hire their first US VP of Sales. The Forum is your safe harbor—a private, encrypted community for honest conversations about the hard things: founder psychology, board management, and the lonely reality of leadership.
